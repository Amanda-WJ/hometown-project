Welcome to New Mills

Local Information

New Mills sits on the edge of the Peak District in the High Peak, Derbyshire. Close to the border of Stockport, Greater Manchester, it has fantastic train links with Manchester, Sheffield and the surrounding areas.

A popular tourist destination for walkers, climbers, hikers and mountain bikers and with a bustling town centre where you will find a multitude of cafes, shops, pubs and restaurants. There is something for everyone here in New Mills.

Events

New Mills hosts many popular events, such as New Mills Festival, which plays host to many fantastic bands, like The Baghdaddies (pictured above), street performers, theatre and arts events every year. Closing with a busy street party, it is one of the highlights of the year.

There is also the locally famous Lantern Parade - which we hope will return this coming September - New Mills Carnival, One World Festival and regular artisan markets and craft fairs at Torr Vale Mill and St Georges Parish Hall. See the dedicated Visit New Mills webpage for more information on local events.

Attractions

There are several popular attractions in New Mills, one of which, the Millennium Walkway, is pictured above. Walking on towards the waterfall you will find The Torrs Hydro, a community owned hydro-electric plant, which provides green energy to many homes in the area.

Being so close to the Peak District means New Mills is a popular destination for outdoor sports. Kinder Scout is just a short distance away in Hayfield and is one of the most popular destinations for hikers in the area. Some spectacular views of Kinder can be seen from many of the hills and viewpoints around New Mills, examples of which can be seen at the top and bottom of this page.
